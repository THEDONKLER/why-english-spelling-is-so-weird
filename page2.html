Why is English Spelling So Weird?
-- the explanation

The main problem with english is that it has never undergone a spelling reform like most other languages. So Modern English spelling is a product of its history

To begin, let's see where english gets its vocabulary from. There are mainly 4 "word stocks".
All with their own DIFFERENT spelling systems.
Here are the 4 word stocks.

A Germanic core (Anglo-Saxon & Old Norse, old english)
French (Norman French/ old french, modern parisian french)
Latin (The Language of Prestige)
Greek (The Language of Knowledge)

When the Anglo-Saxons first began writing in the Latin script rather than their Runic Script, There weren't enough symbols for all the sounds in their language (old english)

To deal with the lack of letters, they invented some letters and gave others more than one purpose.

English entered the Middle english period when the Normans from France came to England. Their Old French language had a big impact on English.

"ch" in old french made the "ch" in modern english but "ch" in modern french makes the "sh" sound in modern english.

so church has a ch sound (as english took it from old french) but champagne makes the "sh" sound because english took it from modern french.

Some more modern vs old French influence gives us "qu" being pronounced differently
scribes thought that the "ch" sound should be "tch" after short vowels. So we have catch and dutch. But not everyone liked this, so we have much and such
More changes like this came about because scribes couldnt stick to one spelling.

THEN THE GREAT VOWEL SHIFT! (1350 - 1700)

For some reason, that we don't know to this day, people just started saying their vowels differently. All long vowels in Middle English became different in Modern English.

To make things worse, right in the middle of the vowel shifting (1400s - 1500s), spelling became more standardised due to the printing press in England.

But the people who worked on the printing press and the standardization of english spelling messed up a lot, but english spelling was frozen in time due to their incompetance.

After the standardization was complete, people stopped saying their consonants too! for example, the k and the gh in knight used to be pronounced! But sometimes the gh had an f like in rough or it disappeared completely like in knight.

After more respelling according to latin roots, we have the mess we call English Spelling.
